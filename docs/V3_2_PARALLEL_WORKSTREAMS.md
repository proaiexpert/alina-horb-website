# V3.2 Parallel Workstreams

This document coordinates non-code work that can proceed while the main chat handles GitHub stabilization and small production fixes.

## Workstream A — Deep Research: article evidence, search intent and safety

Goal: produce a source-grounded research pack for the four initial Notes articles, without editing repository code.

Deliverables for each topic:

1. Primary reader question and search intent in Ukrainian and Russian.
2. Recommended title, H1 and supporting H2/H3 architecture.
3. Reader situations and language that are recognizable but non-diagnostic.
4. Evidence-based factual points supported by authoritative sources.
5. Claims that must be avoided, qualified or referred to medical/crisis services.
6. Recommended internal links to homepage process, FAQ, boundaries and contact.
7. SEO title, meta-description direction and FAQ opportunities.
8. AI-search-friendly definitions and short answer blocks.
9. Source list with citations and publication/update dates.
10. Gaps that require confirmation from Alina.

Initial topics:

- When usual coping methods stop helping.
- What happens during the first consultation.
- How to start the conversation when the request is difficult to formulate.
- Stress, relocation and loss of familiar support.

Research rules:

- Prefer WHO, government/public-health resources, recognized professional associations, universities and peer-reviewed research.
- Do not diagnose readers.
- Do not present the website or form as emergency support.
- Do not invent Alina’s methods, licenses, geography or experience.
- Keep Ukrainian as the primary canonical language; Russian requires independent natural localization.
- Separate stable facts from editorial recommendations.

Final output: one structured report that the main coordinating chat can convert into final article briefs and repository tasks.

## Workstream B — Editorial writing and visual article direction

Goal: transform the existing four article drafts into premium editorial content architecture and prepare a distinct image concept for each article. This work is editorial only; do not change GitHub code.

Deliverables:

1. Audit the existing UA/RU article drafts and Notes index.
2. Rewrite Ukrainian drafts in calm, natural, professional language.
3. Prepare separate natural Russian editorial versions, not literal translations.
4. Remove generic AI phrasing, repetition, empty reassurance and unsupported clinical claims.
5. Strengthen intros, section rhythm, pull quotes, practical observation blocks and conclusions.
6. Propose one distinct premium editorial image concept for every article.
7. Define image composition, aspect ratios, desktop/mobile crops and alt-text direction.
8. Avoid repeating generic vase/branch still lifes.
9. Maintain the Premium Editorial Sanctuary system: Kinfolk editorial hierarchy, Oura restraint and Portland Talk Club clarity.
10. Mark all factual or clinical statements that must be checked against the Deep Research report before publication.

Article image directions should use distinct concepts such as:

- light, paper, notes and interrupted routine;
- two chairs, open notebook or calm consultation setting without a staged therapist/client scene;
- handwritten first lines, folded paper or an unfinished message;
- landscape, packed object, threshold or changing light for relocation and loss of support.

Final output: one UA/RU editorial package plus a four-image art-direction brief. The output remains provisional until reconciled with Workstream A.
